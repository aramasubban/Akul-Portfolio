The Pushcart Project

Social Impact

Created a retrofitted waste pushcart equipped with motors and a battery system to automate manual labor for older workers in a local market in India. Designed and built the solution at age 16 with the goal of reducing physical strain while maintaining familiarity with existing workflows.

Engineering

Conducted analysis on varying road surface conditions to determine motor sizing and battery range requirements. Designed a custom steering rack and developed the overall mechanical layout to ensure stability, maneuverability, and ease of use in dense market environments.

Constraints

Worked within a strict $2,000 budget provided by the local government, requiring cost-efficient component selection and modular design. The solution was intentionally designed to be easily integratable with existing pushcarts to enable scalability and adoption.
