Battery Module Design Contributions

Battery Module – Busbar Design

Designed optimized busbars to minimize overall module length using current density and resistivity calculations. Improved terminal busbar geometry by increasing bend radius to reduce vertical height, resulting in a more compact module.

Implemented a staggered wire bond arrangement to improve manufacturability and reliability for thermistor-based data collection.

Impact: Achieved a lighter, more compact battery module while improving assembly efficiency and electrical reliability.

Battery Module – Spacers and TIM

Ensured no direct contact between cells and the cooling plate while maintaining full compliance with SAE regulations. Conducted thermal conductivity analysis to identify the optimal TIM thickness for efficient heat transfer.

Designed and integrated spacers to maintain uniform thermal contact across the module, improving cooling consistency and manufacturability.

Impact: Improved thermal management, enhancing battery longevity and ensuring stable performance under high-load operating conditions.

Battery Module – Simscape Models

Developing Simulink-based battery models to simulate key parameters including State of Charge (SOC), heat generation (kW), and cell temperature distribution.

Leveraged simulation outputs to refine cooling strategies and guide design decisions for the next iteration of the battery pack prior to physical prototyping.

Impact: Expected to accelerate development cycles and improve cooling efficiency by validating design decisions through predictive modeling.
