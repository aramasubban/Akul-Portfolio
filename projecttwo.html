Custom Phone Controlled Mini-Racecar

Engineering

3D printed and tested the entire chassis and drivetrain, implementing a 5:1 gearing ratio and a custom steering configuration with a dual-motor setup to improve torque performance and low-speed control. Iteratively refined the mechanical layout to balance power delivery, stability, and maneuverability.

Integration

Developed a custom control interface using the Blynk platform to enable remote operation of the motors and servo. Integrated software and hardware systems to achieve precise steering response and controlled power delivery through a phone-based interface.

Constraints

Built the vehicle using existing motors and a servo, designing all mechanical and electrical systems around fixed components. Constrained the entire car within a 10-inch footprint while achieving a 65-degree turning radius, requiring compact packaging and careful system integration.
